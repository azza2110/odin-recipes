Achari Paneer with Tomato Salad and Naan

Description

Pan-fried paneer cooked in a flavourful tomato-based curry, served atop a warm naan and a refreshing tomato and coriander salad.

Ingredients

  • 1 red onion
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 10g coriander
  • 1/2 cucumber
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
  • 35g tomato paste
  • 80g natural yoghurt
  • 1/2 tsp dried chilli flakes
  • 200g paneer
  • 15g fresh ginger
  • 6g vegetable stock
  • 2 plain naans
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • salt
  • pepper
  • vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Preheat the over to 180Â°C, and boil half a kettle
  2. Chop the paneer into large, bite-sized cubes
  3. Heat a drizzle of oil on a large frying pan over medium heat. Once hot, add the paneer cubes and cook for 4 minutes flipping once, and seasoning with a pinch of salt. When golden, set aside on a plate
  4. Peel the red onion. Finely slice 1/4, and finely dice the remaining 3/4
  5. Cut the cucumber in half lengthways, remove the seeds with a spoon, and chop the deseeded cucumber into half moons
  6. Heat a drizzle of oil in the large frying pan over medium heat. Once hot, add the diced red onion with a pinch of salt for 3 minutes or until beginning to soften
  7. Peel and grate the garlic and ginger
  8. Once the onion has softened, add the garlic and ginger to the pan with the ground turmeric, ground cumin, chilli flakes, and a pinch of salt. Cook for 2 minutes or until fragrant
  9. Dissolve the vegetable stock mix in 150mL of boiled water
  10. Make a clearing in the pan, add a drizzle of vegetable oil, and fry the tomato paste for 2 minutes
  11. Add the vegetable stock and sugar and cook for 4 minutes or until thickened slightly
  12. Place the naans on a baking tray, sprinkle with water and salt, and put in oven for 5 minutes to warm through
  13. Finely chop the coriander, including the stalks
  14. Chop the tomatoes into rough, bite-sized pieces
  15. Combine the tomato, cucumber, sliced red onion and a pinch of coriander in a bowl with a drizzle of olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper
  16. Return the paneer to the pan and cook for 1 minutes
  17. Stir in the yoghurt and cook for a further 2 minutes until everything is warmed through
  18. Remove the pan from the heat and stir through half the remaining coriander
  19. Serve the paneer curry over the warmed naan, with the tomato salad on the side. Garnish with remaining coriander
